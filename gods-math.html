The Framework

God's Math.

It disrupts. It breaks the rules. It's mind-boggling. 1 + 1 = 10,000.

A different equation for what's possible. The supernatural formula where divine partnership creates exponential results — and the operating principle behind everything I teach.

What is God's Math?

It's the math that doesn't add up — until it does.

Earthly math says one plus one equals two. Effort plus time equals output. Capital plus labor equals return. The numbers track. The results are predictable.

God's Math doesn't follow that rule. It says one act of obedience can produce a return ten thousand times what you put in. It says the woman who tithes from the bottom can build wealth that no spreadsheet predicted. It says the small business with the right partnership outpaces the funded one with the right consultant.

That's the framework I run my businesses on. Not in place of strategy — alongside it. Not against the work — through the work. Every system, every hire, every offer is built knowing that the math is supposed to break.

The Principles

Four ways the math breaks.

+

Multiplication where there should be addition.

When you partner with God in your work, the output exceeds the input. The hours you put in get multiplied. The effort gets stretched further than it should. Every CEO I know who operates this way says the same thing: I cannot explain how this got built.

−

Subtraction that creates abundance.

Tithing. Sabbath. Saying no. The world calls them losses. God's Math calls them deposits. Every time you give back what was given, the next deposit comes bigger. The math runs in reverse — and somehow you end up with more.

×

Compound returns on obedience.

Small acts of faithfulness compound. The hire you didn't see coming. The client referral that opens a door. The decision you made when no one was watching that pays off three years later. The math is patient. It's also unstoppable.

=

A different definition of enough.

The world tells you what success looks like. God's Math redefines the equation entirely. Enough isn't a number — it's a posture. And the moment you stop chasing the wrong sum, the right one tends to arrive.

Every business I've built was built on the math that didn't add up.
And it kept adding up anyway.

In Practice

What this looks like inside a real business.

God's Math is not a theory I teach. It's the operating system behind every decision I've made — and every result I've watched unfold because of it.

i

Hiring by alignment, not by résumé.

The best hires I ever made were not the most credentialed ones on paper. They were the ones the framework kept pointing to. My COO Tristan was my first hire — and is still here years later because the alignment was right from day one.

ii

Pricing from value, not from fear.

Earthly math says price low to win volume. God's Math says price what the work is worth and trust the right clients to find you. Every time I raised prices in obedience to that, the right people showed up.

iii

Scaling through release, not through grip.

I scaled from one staff member to thirteen by letting go — of control, of the impulse to do everything myself, of the version of leadership that thinks holding tighter creates more. The looser I held, the bigger it got.

iv

Stewardship as the operating principle.

Everything I've built belongs to God before it belongs to me. That single posture changes how you spend, how you give, how you recover from a hard quarter, and how you celebrate a good one. The math becomes about faithfulness — and the returns follow.

The math is supposed to break.
